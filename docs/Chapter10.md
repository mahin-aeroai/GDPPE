**GLOBAL DIGITAL PRINT PRODUCTION EQUIPMENT ENCYCLOPEDIA (GDPPE)**

**Master Research & Data Collection Specification**

**Chapter 10 -- Engineering Data Collection Standards by Equipment
Category**

**10.1 Purpose**

The objective of this chapter is to define **how engineering data shall
be collected** for every equipment category covered by the Global
Digital Print Production Equipment Encyclopedia (GDPPE).

Unlike conventional catalogs that merely reproduce manufacturer
brochures, GDPPE aims to collect engineering data that is meaningful
for:

-   Production Planning

-   Factory Layout

-   Machine Comparison

-   Procurement

-   Service Engineering

-   Preventive Maintenance

-   Process Optimization

-   Color Management

-   Quality Assurance

-   Automation Integration

-   AI-based Knowledge Graphs

Each technology requires its own engineering methodology.

This chapter establishes **mandatory**, **recommended**,
and **optional** engineering parameters for every category.

**10.2 Engineering Data Classification**

Every technical field shall belong to one of four research levels.

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Level**   **Description**                              **Priority**
  ----------- -------------------------------------------- --------------
  Level 1     Mandatory Engineering Data                   ★★★★★

  Level 2     Recommended Engineering Data                 ★★★★☆

  Level 3     Advanced Engineering Data                    ★★★☆☆

  Level 4     Expert / Factory Research                    ★★☆☆☆
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------

**Level 1 (Mandatory)**

Must be available for every equipment record whenever publicly
published.

Examples

-   Machine Width

-   Machine Weight

-   Print Width

-   Printheads

-   Voltage

-   Speed

**Level 2 (Recommended)**

Collect whenever available.

Examples

-   Servo Motors

-   PLC

-   Vacuum Pump

-   Cooling System

-   Automation Features

**Level 3 (Advanced)**

Usually available from manuals.

Examples

-   Linear Guides

-   Bearings

-   Encoder Resolution

-   Controller Type

**Level 4 (Factory Research)**

Normally obtained from service manuals or direct manufacturer
interaction.

Examples

-   PCB Architecture

-   Internal Wiring

-   Servo Tuning

-   PLC Ladder

-   Factory Calibration

**10.6 Data Availability Classification**

Not every engineering field is publicly available.

Each parameter shall be classified as:

  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Class**   **Meaning**
  ----------- -----------------------------------------------------------------
  P           Publicly Available

  D           Dealer / Distributor Information

  M           Manual / Service Documentation

  F           Factory / OEM Only

  E           Estimated from Multiple Sources (must be clearly marked as
              editorial, not fact)
  -----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Researchers should always prioritize P, D, and M sources. Factory-only
information should never be guessed.

**10.8 Engineering Notes vs. Verified Specifications**

GDPPE shall distinguish between:

**Verified Specification**

Published and traceable to an authoritative source.

Example:

Maximum media thickness: **50 mm** (Official Technical Datasheet, Rev.
2025)

**Editorial Engineering Note**

Expert commentary or synthesis based on experience.

Example:

The vacuum zoning design is particularly effective for mixed-media
production but may require additional masking when processing narrow
substrates.

Editorial notes must never be presented as manufacturer specifications.

**10.10 Chapter Summary**

This chapter defines the operational engineering standards for
researching equipment across all GDPPE categories. By combining a
universal engineering framework with technology-specific templates, the
encyclopedia captures meaningful technical information without forcing
unrelated parameters into a generic structure. It also distinguishes
verified specifications from editorial engineering analysis and
introduces completeness scoring, enabling consistent, high-quality
research across thousands of products.
